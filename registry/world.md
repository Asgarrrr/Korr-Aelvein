# The world

## The abyss

A vast and **inexplicable** abyss at the heart of an island. Its mere presence affects the whole world for reasons no one understands. No one knows:

- what it is
- why it is there
- what lies at the bottom
- how long it has existed

The abyss has **a life of its own**. The things that dwell within it — monsters, creatures, perhaps more — hunt and devour each other. The victor of an encounter *grows stronger* (sim mechanic, to be specified in `systems.md`). Sometimes, a creature grown strong enough rises through the upper floors, then surfaces and threatens the town.

## The town

A town encircles the rim of the abyss. People live there, work there, descend from there. The **métier** (profession) is central — not an RPG class, but a social and economic role.

Candidate métiers (to enrich and finalize): blacksmith, herbalist, hunter, carpenter, weaver, scout, midwife, storyteller, gravedigger, priest, beggar, child. The métier determines *how* the player engages with the abyss, not their combat stats.

## The moral climate

**No calendar. No timer.** Danger simmers in the background. The player reads **diegetic** signs:

- Gossip at the market
- Tracks in the snow
- Scouts who do not return
- Sounds at night
- Something strange at the edge of the village
- The weaver who now only makes shrouds

Engagement is **invited, not imposed**. No one tells the player "MAIN QUEST: GO INTO ABYSS". Doing nothing is a valid option — but the town shrinks.

## The chain

The player **can die** (in the abyss, or during an incursion). On their death, another villager takes over. Not necessarily a relative, not necessarily an heir — just *someone else* from the town.

The body of the previous villager remains **where they fell**: lootable, readable (if they kept a journal), buriable by those who descend deep enough to find it.

The consequence: *village-level progression* is what accumulates across generations. The player is mortal; the town is what persists (until it falls). See `systems.md` for the exact transmission mechanic.

## The end (or its absence)

The game has **no required ending**. The abyss evolves slowly. Things are hidden within it. Several paths to *finishing it* exist — but long-term and optional. The story told is the one your town lives.

## Tone and references

Tonal references (a mix, nothing identical):

- *Pathologic* — moral climate, ambient threat, the feeling that the world exists without you
- *Outer Wilds* — hidden things, optional endings, autonomous world that doesn't wait for you
- *Don't Starve* — villager survival cycle, fragility

Format: 2D grid roguelike, server-authoritative (the abyss simulation runs server-side, not client-side).
